About this Module

A comprehensive guide on transforming lesson plans from "To-Do lists" into meaningful learning journeys using tiered success criteria.

The Journey Metaphor

Think of every lesson as a journey. The Objective is the destination (where we are going), and the Success Criteria are the signposts/maps along the way (how we know we've arrived). Without clear criteria, students are just wandering.

üß†

Objectives ‚â† To-Do Lists

A strong objective defines a cognitive skill (e.g., "Analyze," "Create," "Evaluate"), not just an activity.

Weak: "Complete worksheet page 10."
Strong: "Apply understanding of place value to solve real-world problems."

Differentiation via Tiered Criteria

One destination, multiple paths. Allow students to access the learning at their level.

1

Use physical models/manipulatives to represent the problem.

2

Level 2 (Core)

Independently solve problems using visual models and standard algorithms.

3

Level 3 (Challenge)

Solve complex problems and explain reasoning/methodology to peers.
